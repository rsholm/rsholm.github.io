# Account and Data Deletion

**Effective date:** June 20, 2026

Daily Calendar does not create or manage separate in-app user accounts. Users connect the app to their Google account using Google OAuth so the app can read calendar events and display the user's daily schedule.

## Delete or disconnect your account access

To disconnect Daily Calendar from your Google account, revoke the app's access from your Google Account permissions page:

1. Go to [https://myaccount.google.com/permissions](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions)
2. Sign in to the Google account you used with Daily Calendar.
3. Find **Daily Calendar** in the list of apps or services with account access.
4. Select **Daily Calendar**.
5. Choose **Remove Access**.

After access is removed, Daily Calendar will no longer be authorized to read your Google Calendar data from that Google account.

## Delete local app data from your device

Daily Calendar stores basic app preferences locally on your device, such as theme and display mode settings. To remove local app data:

1. Open Android **Settings**.
2. Go to **Apps** or **Apps & notifications**.
3. Select **Daily Calendar**.
4. Open **Storage**.
5. Tap **Clear storage** or **Clear data**.

You can also uninstall Daily Calendar from your device to remove the app and its locally stored app data.

## Calendar data

Daily Calendar uses read-only Google Calendar access to display calendar event titles, event times, and all-day event status inside the app.

Daily Calendar does not create, edit, or delete Google Calendar events. Daily Calendar does not operate a backend server for storing users' calendar data.
